# Finite Kripke models and provability interpretations in quantified modal logic 

## Haruka Kogure ‚àó‚ÄÝ and Taishi Kurahashi ‚Ä°¬ß 

Abstract 

In this paper, we investigate arithmetical completeness with respect to finite Kripke models of quantified modal logic. We adapt the finite-model embedding techniques of Artemov and Japaridze to two settings involving finite Kripke models. First, for conversely well-founded finite Kripke models of quantified modal logic, we construct a Œ£ 2 Fefermanian provability predicate together with an arithmetical interpretation that embeds the model into arithmetic. Second, for finite constant domain Kripke models of quantified modal logic, we construct a Œ£ 1 provability predicate satisfying D2 G and an arithmetical interpretation yielding such an embedding. 

# 1 Introduction 

Solovay‚Äôs arithmetical completeness theorem [24], which states that the modal propositional logic GL coincides exactly with the provability logic of PA , has become a fundamental cornerstone in the study of provability predicates via modal logic. In particular, the method of embedding transitive and conversely well-founded finite Kripke frames into arithmetic by means of the so-called Solo-vay function remains a central and indispensable technique to this day (see the survey papers [13, 3] on provability logic for several applications of this method). In recent years, the authors have established arithmetical completeness results for K and for several non-normal modal logics. For instance, the arithmeti-cal completeness theorem for the modal propositional logic K with respect to Œ£2 Fefermanian provability predicates was established in [16]. The key tech-nique used in its proof is again an application of Solovay-style constructions. In addition, the arithmetical completeness theorem for K with respect to Œ£ 1

provability predicates was proved in [20]. An overview of recent studies on arithmetical completeness for several logics is given in [14].

It is natural to attempt to extend this framework from propositional logic to predicate logic. However, it has been shown that many of the positive results obtained in the propositional setting do not extend to the predicate case. For example, Montagna [23] proved that the predicate extension QGL of GL is nei-ther Kripke complete nor arithmetically complete. Artemov [1] proved that the quantified truth provability logic of PA is not arithmetical, and Vardanyan [25] proved that the quantified provability logic of PA is ŒÝ 02-complete (see also related results by Berarducci [6], Boolos and McGee [7], and McGee [22]). Furthermore, Artemov [2] showed that the quantified provability logic of PA depends on the choice of formulas representing PA (see also [15, 19] for related results). These results indicate that, unlike in the propositional case, it is nontrivial to control quantified provability logic in a systematic way. On the other hand, some positive results have been obtained. Among them, in this paper, we focus on the work of Artemov and Dzhaparidze, currently spelled Japaridze [4]. They showed that, for finite Kripke models, Solovay‚Äôs method can be extended from propositional logic to predicate logic. More pre-cisely, they proved that for every transitive and conversely well-founded Kripke model of quantified modal logic with finitely many worlds and finite domains, and for every formula A that is not valid in that model, there exists an arith-metical interpretation f such that PA ‚ä¨ f (A). Moreover, Japaridze [8] further refined this result by providing more explicit sufficient conditions under which a Kripke model can be embedded into arithmetic. The aim of the present paper is to combine the method of Artemov and Japaridze [4] with the results on K obtained in [16, 20]. Our main results are as follows. 1. For each conversely well-founded finite Kripke model of quantified modal logic, there exist a Œ£ 2 Fefermanian provability predicate of PA and a corre-sponding arithmetical interpretation such that the model can be embedded into arithmetic (Theorem 3.2). 2. For each finite constant domain Kripke model of quantified modal logic, there exist a Œ£ 1 provability predicate of PA satisfying the global version 

D2 G of the derivability condition D2 and a corresponding arithmetical interpretation such that the model can be embedded into arithmetic (The-orem 4.1). The first result can be obtained by a straightforward combination of the ar-guments of Artemov and Japaridze with those of [16]. Therefore, the technical contribution of this paper lies in the second result. Unlike the first result, it can-not be obtained merely by combining existing arguments. In order to connect quantified modal logic with arithmetic, one must overcome substantial difficul-ties arising from the treatment of quantifiers and substitutions of numerals. Our proof introduces essentially new ideas to address these issues. The paper is organized as follows. In Section 2, we introduce preliminaries on quantified modal logic, provability predicates, and arithmetical interpreta-tions. In Section 3, we prove the first main theorem stating that conversely 2well-founded finite Kripke models can be embedded into arithmetic by using Œ£ 2

Fefermanian provability predicates. In Section 4, we establish our second main result stating that finite constant domain Kripke models can be embedded into arithmetic by means of Œ£ 1 provability predicates satisfying D2 G. Finally, in the Appendix, we prove the primitive recursiveness of the function h, which is used in the construction of the provability predicate in Section 4. The method devel-oped in this appendix is expected to be useful in the construction of provability predicates whose behavior essentially involves free variables and substitutions.

In this section, we prove that for any finite constant domain Kripke model of quantified modal logic, there exists a Œ£ 1 provability predicate Pr T (x) of T

satisfying D2 G and an arithmetical interpretation based on Pr T (x) such that the model can be embedded into arithmetic. The main difference from the result in Section 3 is that the provability predicate Pr T (x) obtained in this section is not Fefermanian. As discussed in Subsection 2.3, the provability predicate constructed here does not necessarily satisfy D1 U. Another important point is that, unlike in Section 3, we assume that Kripke models have constant domains rather than being conversely well-founded. In [20], a method was developed for embedding arbitrary finite Kripke models of propositional modal logic into arithmetic by using Œ£ 1 provability predicates. However, this method cannot be directly extended to quantified modal logic. The reason is that, when considering arithmetical interpretations of quantified modal logic, one must carefully deal with free variables and substitutions of 11 numerals. To carry out the argument of [20] in this setting, the provability predicate would be required to satisfy both D1 U and D2 U. However, the provability predicates we aim to construct cannot be guaranteed to satisfy both of these conditions as we mentioned in Subsection 2.3. Therefore, it is necessary to develop a different proof method. Our strategy is as follows. We first construct a function h corresponding to a Solovay function. The function h starts with h(0) = 0 and makes at most one transition to a nonzero value. We then define Œª(x) by ‚àÉy (h(y) = x). In contrast to the construction of Artemov and Japaridze [4], we can define the formulas Œ∏k(x) in a simpler way by using the assumption that the given finite Kripke frame is constant domain. A crucial point is that, although the definition of h involves complicated conditions related to quantifiers, substitutions, and tautological consequence, the function h is in fact primitive recursive. This fact plays a key role in our proof. Since the proof of this fact is somewhat lengthy, we postpone it to the Appendix. As a consequence, Œª(x) is a Œ£ 1 formula.
