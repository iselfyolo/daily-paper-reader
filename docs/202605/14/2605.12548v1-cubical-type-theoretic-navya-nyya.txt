Abstract 

We present a formalization of the technical language of Navya-Ny¬Į aya ‚ÄĒ the ‚ÄúNew Logic‚ÄĚ school of late-classical Indian philosophy ‚ÄĒ in CCHM De Morgan cubical type theory (CTT). Previous formalization attempts in first-order logic (Matilal), higher-order logic (Ganeri), and Martin-L√∂f type theory (Bhattacharyya) each lose load-bearing structure: dependent delimitation ( avacchedaka ), typed absence ( abh¬Į ava ), non-extensional identity ( t¬Į ad¬Į atmya ), or unbounded relational depth ( parampar¬Į a-sambandha ). We argue that CTT closes this gap natively. We give CTT encodings for seven core constructs ( sambandha , avacchedaka ,

abh¬Į ava , vy¬Į apti , t¬Į ad¬Į atmya , higher relations, pary¬Į apti ) plus the qualifier-qualificand structure; develop a stratified-universe foundation for the pad¬Į artha system; and prove four signa-ture theorems internal to the encoding (Involution of abh¬Į ava , Keval¬Į anvayi -irreducibility, Coextension-without-identity, No-h-set-collapse) and six metatheoretic results (soundness, conservativity, faithfulness, distinction-preservation, decidability, commentarial conserva-tivity). We close with worked encodings of fifteen Tattvacint¬Į ama n. i passages, comparison with prior formalizations, an implementation sketch in Cubical Agda, and five distinguishing predictions ‚ÄĒ including a novel argument from Navya-Ny¬Į aya‚Äôs involutive-negation doctrine for the necessity of De Morgan over Cartesian cubical foundations. 

Keywords: Navya-Ny¬Į aya, cubical type theory, formal philosophy, dependent types, higher inductive types, autoformalization.

1.3 Why cubical type theory 

Cubical type theory in the CCHM presentation [ 9] arose from a different motivating problem: making Voevodsky‚Äôs univalence axiom compute . The argument that Navya-Ny¬Į aya‚Äôs abh¬Į ava -of-abh¬Į ava doctrine selects CCHM over Cartesian cubical is, to our knowledge, the first principled argument from a non-mathematical tradition for choosing between cubical foundations. # 20 Conclusion 

We have argued that CCHM De Morgan cubical type theory closes the autoformalization gap that previous attempts could not close. The argument runs along several converging lines: the structural fit between Navya-Ny¬Į aya‚Äôs relational vocabulary and CTT‚Äôs path-typing; the recovery of dependent delimitation, typed absence, and intensional identity; the metatheoretic results of ¬ß14; and the philosophical convergence of ¬ß17. The result is of interest beyond the narrow project of Navya-Ny¬Į aya formalization. It illustrates a more general phenomenon: that pre-modern philosophical traditions developed expressive demands that twentieth-century logic struggled to meet, and that twenty-first-century type theory ‚ÄĒ developed for entirely different reasons ‚ÄĒ has incidentally supplied the needed structure. Where this convergence holds, the tradition‚Äôs formalization is not a forcing but a natural fit. The deeper question ‚ÄĒ whether CCHM is the foundational system for relational logic generally, or merely one fit among possible alternatives ‚ÄĒ is open. We have not argued for the strong claim. What we have shown is that for one fourteenth-century tradition with documented metatheoretic commitments, CCHM is uniquely faithful. Whether other traditions force the same answer or different ones is a project we leave to future work.
